# Product Direction

## Positioning

Duc should be positioned as:

> AI Product Engineer / Applied AI Engineer

Core narrative:

> Duc builds measurable, traceable, production-oriented AI products from ambiguous workflows to deployable systems.

The site should show a builder who can connect product discovery, system design, backend engineering, retrieval/model pipelines, evaluation, deployment, observability, and iteration.

## Audience

Primary audiences:
- AI engineering recruiters.
- Backend engineering recruiters.
- Applied AI teams.
- AI product teams.
- Startup founders.
- Forward-deployed engineering teams.
- Technical hiring managers.

The site must answer within 20 to 30 seconds:
1. What problems does Duc solve?
2. What has he actually built?
3. What evidence supports the claims?
4. Can he take a product from ambiguity to deployment?
5. Where can someone inspect the work or contact him?

## Messaging Pillars

1. Product clarity
   - He starts with users, workflow constraints, and success criteria.
   - Evidence: Legolas discovery, user story, PRD, worklog, wireframe/UX flow, Demo Day planning.

2. Applied AI systems
   - He builds grounded AI workflows, not only model demos.
   - Evidence: Legolas Q&A, drafting, review, citation grounding, guardrails, evaluation.

3. Backend and production readiness
   - He handles APIs, auth, persistence, cloud topology, deployment constraints, and observability.
   - Evidence: FastAPI, PostgreSQL/Supabase, Render, Hugging Face Space, Vercel, Langfuse, runbooks.

4. Evaluation and traceability
   - He measures retrieval and AI workflow quality instead of relying on demos alone.
   - Evidence: 30-case benchmark, Legal Zalo 2021 retrieval evaluation, hybrid retrieval docs.

5. Research to product
   - He can work with world models and representation learning, then connect that depth to product work.
   - Evidence: HanoiWorld CV description and public GitHub link.

## Editorial Voice

Use:
- Specific nouns.
- Evidence-backed claims.
- Contribution boundaries.
- Trade-offs and lessons.
- Calm confidence.

Avoid:
- Passionate developer.
- AI enthusiast.
- Cutting-edge solutions.
- Revolutionizing the future.
- Expert/mastered.
- Generic framework lists.
- Overstated seniority.

## Public Contribution Boundaries

Legolas AI was a 2-person team project. Public copy must say:
- "Co-developed" for team-owned outcomes.
- "Owned" only for items supported by worklog/CV.
- "Contributed to" for shared architecture and workflows.

HanoiWorld was a group project. Public copy must say:
- "Group project."
- "Implemented/contributed to" only where supported by CV.

Powfolio appears to be Duc's product/tooling project, but public validation is not proven. Say:
- "Currently building."
- "MVP CLI."
- "Tested local pipeline."
- Avoid "used by developers" unless verified later.

## Day 0 Product Decision

Build a portfolio that is:
- Evidence-first.
- Data-driven and maintainable.
- Visually premium but restrained.
- Fast, accessible, and Vercel-ready.
- Focused on selected work, not page count.

Do not build:
- A generic student portfolio.
- A framework gallery.
- A futuristic AI-art landing page.
- A portfolio-builder demo about building portfolios.
